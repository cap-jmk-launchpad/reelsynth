\begin{abstract}
Periodic wavetable frames with open wrap seams $x[0]\neq x[N-1]$ induce audible
\emph{crackle} under cyclic playback. We treat wrap denoising as an
\emph{unsupervised} optimization problem: a seam-local periodize stack with
parameters $\theta\in[0,1]^{12}$ is fit by minimizing a joint loss of
\textbf{denoise gain} and \textbf{mid-cycle shape retention}, without paired
clean/noisy labels. We construct a procedural \textbf{100\,000}-cycle bench
spanning morphs, FFT-style harmonic overlays, mix modes, and forced cliffs;
run coordinate-descent fit; then apply an outer \textbf{meta-learning /
hyperparameter} loop over $\lambda$, fade/polish biases, and training budgets.
The champion recipe (\texttt{fft\_overlay\_specialist}) locks frozen $\theta^\star$
for $O(N)$ inference as ReelSynth \texttt{Seam·Opt}. On held-out validation,
quality $Q=\tfrac12(\mathcal{D}+\mathcal{S})$ reaches $\approx 0.791$, matching or
exceeding DualCosine while keeping $\mathcal{S}\approx 0.99$. We situate the
method relative to classical VA anti-aliasing (BLEP/BLAMP), wavetable DSP, and
label-free audio restoration literature.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Wavetable synthesizers store one-period waveforms and advance a phase accumulator
modulo~$1$. If the stored cycle is not continuous at the wrap, each period
boundary injects a broadband transient. Classical remedies include linear
detrend, raised-cosine fades, and bandlimited steps (BLEP) for geometric
oscillators~\cite{valimaki2006va,esqueda2016aliasing}. Quantized / edited
wavetable frames, however, often need a \emph{bake-time} periodizer that (i)
closes wrap, (ii) preserves mid-cycle timbre, and (iii) remains $O(N)$ for UI
rebuilds.

We contribute: (1)~a measurable crackle functional $C(\cdot)$ and joint loss
$L=(1-\mathcal{D})+\lambda(1-\mathcal{S})$; (2)~DenoiseOpt, a moderately deep
seam-local stack with shape invariant mid-cycle copy; (3)~a 100k procedural
dataset with extreme harmonic overlays; (4)~meta-learning over hyperparameters
to select a champion $\theta^\star$; (5)~empirical figures and an open
implementation in ReelSynth.

\section{Related work}

\paragraph{Virtual-analog anti-aliasing.}
PolyBLEP / BLAMP methods suppress aliasing from geometric discontinuities in
VA oscillators~\cite{valimaki2006va,esqueda2016blamp,esqueda2016aliasing}.
These operate on continuous-time event models; our target is discrete
\emph{stored} cycles after user edits.

\paragraph{Wavetable DSP.}
Integrated / higher-order wavetable methods and differentiable wavetable
synthesis improve interpolation and learnable tables~\cite{idealintegrator2013,shan2021dwt,ddsp_review2024}.
We instead optimize a small periodize operator for wrap character.

\paragraph{Label-free / self-supervised audio restoration.}
Unpaired denoising and self-supervised restoration avoid clean targets by
exploiting measurable structure~\cite{unsupervised_audio_note}. DenoiseOpt is
analogous at cycle scale: the ``noise'' is the wrap cliff, scored by $C(\cdot)$.

\paragraph{Meta-learning \& HPO in audio.}
Outer-loop search over training knobs is standard in AutoML; we apply a
lightweight grid + named priors over $\lambda$ and seam biases, evaluating on
held-out procedural seeds.

\section{Problem formulation}

Let $r\in\mathbb{R}^N$ be a raw cycle and $y=f_\theta(r)$ the periodized output.
Define
\begin{align}
C(x) &= 2\,\mathrm{wrap}(x)+\mathrm{max\_step}(x)+0.35\,\mathrm{hf}(x),\\
\mathcal{D}(r,y) &= \mathrm{clamp}\!\Big(\frac{C(r)-C(y)}{\max(C(r),\varepsilon)},0,1\Big),\\
\mathcal{S}(r,y) &= 1-\mathrm{clamp}\!\Big(\frac{\mathrm{MAE}_{\mathrm{mid}}(y,r)}{\mathrm{RMS}(r)+\varepsilon},0,1\Big),\\
L &= (1-\mathcal{D})+\lambda(1-\mathcal{S}),\qquad
Q=\tfrac12(\mathcal{D}+\mathcal{S}).
\end{align}
Mid-band excludes the outer $N/8$ samples so legitimate seam edits are not
penalized as shape loss. \textbf{Shape invariant:} $y[i]=r[i]$ for
$i\notin$ fade zones.

\section{Method}

\subsection{DenoiseOpt stack}
Five seam stages controlled by $\theta$: soft pull to a closed target, asymmetric
dual-end fade, ease mix (smoothstep / raised-cosine) with gamma, secondary tail
fade, dual 3-tap polish + wrap pin (Fig.~\ref{fig:pipeline}).

\subsection{100k bench (dataset)}
Seeds $0..99999$ generate ten families (10k each, $N=256$): morph, harmonic\_fft,
extreme\_overlay, combo (Add/Avg/invert/half), quant\_steps, nonlinear,
am\_fm, noise\_tone, triple\_mix, open\_wrap\_bias. Harmonic families synthesize
FFT-style additive spectra with optional extreme high-partial tilt and forced
cliffs---a diverse unlabeled corpus for fit + investigation.

\subsection{Fit and meta-learning}
Inner loop: coordinate descent on $\theta$ (random restarts). Outer loop: 28
hyperparameter trials over $\lambda\in\{0.5,1,1.5,2\}$, fade/polish/pin biases,
and named recipes (\texttt{aggressive\_denoise}, \texttt{shape\_first},
\texttt{balanced\_meta}, \texttt{fft\_overlay\_specialist}). Meta objective:
maximize $Q$ subject to $\mathcal{S}\ge 0.97$ on 1500 held-out seeds.

\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Baselines}
On validation, DualCosine $Q\approx 0.788$, prior DenoiseOpt $Q\approx 0.790$,
classic $Q\approx 0.772$. EnsembleV3 collapses shape ($\mathcal{S}\approx 0.34$)
and is rejected. Meta champion \texttt{fft\_overlay\_specialist}:
$\mathcal{D}\approx 0.590$, $\mathcal{S}\approx 0.992$, $Q\approx 0.791$
(Fig.~\ref{fig:baselines}, Fig.~\ref{fig:pareto}).

\section{Discussion}
Seam-local depth + explicit $\mathcal{S}$ yields aggressive denoise without
flattening musical mid-cycle content. Meta-learning shifts $\theta$ toward
overlay-heavy regimes without sacrificing the shape floor. Residual crackle in
combo/nonlinear families is expected: interior edges need BLEP-class live
processing~\cite{valimaki2006va}, not bake periodize alone.

\section{Limitations}
Bake metrics $\neq$ live BLEP; hosted \texttt{research.klaut.pro} edge routing
was unavailable during writing---literature harvested via local Klaut Research
MCP (OpenAlex/Crossref/arXiv). Fit is bench-specific; retune with
\texttt{bench\_denoise\_meta}.

\section{Conclusion}
DenoiseOpt casts wavetable wrap crackle as label-free optimization of a
measurable loss, scaled on a 100k procedural bench and refined by
meta-learning. Frozen $\theta^\star$ ships as a fast inference option in
ReelSynth.
